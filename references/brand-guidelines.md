## 1. Brand Overview

**Centricity Research** is one of the largest clinical research networks in North America, operating research sites across the United States and Canada. We connect patients with clinical trials and advance medicine through accessible, community-based research.

### Mission

Advancing medicine through accessible, community-based clinical research.

### What We Do

- Conduct clinical trials across a broad range of therapeutic areas
- Connect patients with research studies in their local communities
- Partner with pharmaceutical sponsors to deliver high-quality, enrollment-ready trial sites
- Operate a distributed network of research sites embedded in the communities they serve

### Brand Promise

Every person deserves access to tomorrow's medicine today — and every community deserves a trusted partner in that journey.

### Brand Pillars

| Pillar | Description |
|---|---|
| **Access** | Research should be local, convenient, and open to everyone |
| **Trust** | Patients and sponsors rely on our integrity, transparency, and rigor |
| **Community** | We are part of the neighborhoods we serve, not outside them |
| **Impact** | Every study we run brings medicine one step closer to the people who need it |

## 2. Brand Archetype: The Trusted Guide

Centricity Research embodies **The Trusted Guide** — a healthcare authority that combines deep expertise with warmth and accessibility. We are not a distant institution handing down knowledge; we are a knowledgeable partner walking alongside patients, sponsors, and communities on the path to better health outcomes.

### Core Traits

| Trait | What It Means | How It Shows Up |
|---|---|---|
| **Trust & Safety** | Patients trust us with their health; credibility is paramount | Transparent communication, clear consent processes, consistent follow-through |
| **Accessibility** | Clinical research should be approachable, not intimidating | Plain language, welcoming imagery, local site presence, simple enrollment flows |
| **Community** | Local sites embedded in real communities, not a faceless corporation | Site-specific content, community photography, local staff profiles |
| **Scientific Rigor** | Evidence-based, thorough, precise in everything we do | Data-backed claims, proper citations, methodical processes |
| **Empowerment** | Helping patients take an active role in their health journey | Patient-centered language, education-first content, celebrating participant impact |

### Archetype in Practice

- We **guide**, not lecture. We make complex research processes understandable.
- We **invite**, not recruit. Participation is a choice we honor, not a quota we fill.
- We **reassure**, not dismiss. Patient concerns are valid and addressed with care.
- We **demonstrate**, not assert. Our credibility comes from our track record, not our claims.

## 3. Brand Voice

Centricity Research speaks as an institution with warmth — professional but never cold, knowledgeable but never condescending. We do not use a named persona or character. The voice should feel like a trusted healthcare professional who genuinely cares about the person in front of them.

### Tone Guidelines

| Principle | Do | Don't |
|---|---|---|
| **Warm but professional** | "We're here to answer your questions at every step." | "Hey! Got questions? Hit us up!" |
| **Clear and direct** | "This study involves 4 visits over 12 weeks." | "Participation may entail a series of periodic consultations over a multi-week timeframe." |
| **Empowering** | "You can make a difference in the future of heart disease treatment." | "We need subjects for our cardiac trial." |
| **Trustworthy** | "In our network, 94% of participants say they felt safe and informed." | "We're the best research network out there." |
| **Community-oriented** | "Your local research site in Tampa is enrolling now." | "Our facility is accepting applicants." |
| **Inclusive** | "Clinical trials need people of all backgrounds to ensure treatments work for everyone." | "Minorities are underrepresented in trials." |

### Audience-Specific Voice

#### Patients & Participants

- **Tone**: Warm, encouraging, reassuring
- **Language**: Plain language (aim for 6th–8th grade reading level), no unexplained acronyms
- **Focus**: Their health journey, their agency, their safety
- **Key messages**: "You're in control." / "Your participation matters." / "We're with you every step."

**Example — Patient-facing copy:**
> Joining a clinical trial is a personal decision, and we respect that. Our team will walk you through every detail — what to expect, how long it takes, and how your safety is protected — so you can decide what's right for you.

#### Sponsors & Pharmaceutical Partners

- **Tone**: Professional, confident, data-driven
- **Language**: Industry terminology is appropriate; emphasize metrics, capabilities, and network scale
- **Focus**: Enrollment speed, data quality, site infrastructure, geographic reach
- **Key messages**: "Proven enrollment capabilities." / "Trusted by leading sponsors." / "Data you can rely on."

**Example — Sponsor-facing copy:**
> With 60+ active sites across North America and a proprietary patient database of 2M+ individuals, Centricity Research delivers enrollment velocity and data integrity at scale. Our median site activation time is 30% faster than the industry benchmark.

### Language to Avoid

| Never Say | Say Instead |
|---|---|
| "Guinea pig" or "test subject" | "Participant" or "volunteer" |
| "Experimental drug" (without context) | "Investigational treatment" or "study medication" |
| "We guarantee results" | "Research has shown promising outcomes" |
| "You should be scared of [condition]" | "If you're living with [condition], new options may be available" |
| "Our facility" | "Your local research site" |
| "Subjects needed" | "Now enrolling participants" |
| "Side effects are rare" (unless backed by data) | "Your safety is monitored throughout the study" |

### Grammar & Style Notes

- Use the **Oxford comma** (patients, sponsors, and communities)
- Use **sentence case** for all headings and titles
- Spell out numbers one through nine; use numerals for 10+
- Use **active voice** whenever possible
- Avoid exclamation marks in clinical/medical content
- One space after periods

- Blue-500 on white passes AA for large text; pair with dark blue-800 text for small body copy
- Blue-500 on white does NOT pass AA for small body text — pair with darker text colors for small copy
- Always test color combinations before production use

### Typography Rules

1. **Sentence case on all headings** — never Title Case, never ALL CAPS on headings
2. **ALL CAPS restricted to**: Overline, Label, and Button styles only
3. **Never use Montserrat for body text** — body copy is always Noto Sans
4. **Never use Noto Sans for Display, H1, or H2** — large headings are always Montserrat
5. **Paragraph spacing**: 24px (`1.5rem`) between consecutive `<p>` elements in body content
6. **Maximum line length**: 680px (approximately 65–75 characters) for body text readability
7. **Link text**: Noto Sans, same size as surrounding body, blue-500, underline on hover
8. **Bold body text**: Noto Sans Semi-Bold (600) — never use Bold (700) for body; reserve 700 for headings
9. **Numeric data in tables**: Noto Sans Medium (500), tabular-nums feature enabled

## 6. Logo Usage

### Logo Variants

| Variant | Description | Use When |
|---|---|---|
| **Horizontal (primary)** | Full wordmark — "CENTRICITY Research" with the overlapping circles logomark to the left | Default usage: website headers, documents, letterheads, presentations |
| **Logomark only** | The overlapping circles icon without text | Favicons, app icons, small spaces (< 120px wide), social media avatars, watermarks |
| **White on dark** | White version of both horizontal and logomark variants | Dark backgrounds: dark blue, blue, photography overlays, hero sections |
| **Color on light** | Full-color version | Light backgrounds: white, neutral-50, neutral-100 |

### Logo Colors (Brand Palette)

The logomark's overlapping circles use three colors that are also the core brand UI palette:

| Element | Hex | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Blue circle | `#1E67B2` | Left circle — also the primary brand color |
| Green circle | `#81BF41` | Right circle — also the tertiary brand color |
| Teal overlap | `#10BAB3` | Intersection of the two circles — also the secondary brand color |

These logo colors ARE the brand palette. Blue (`#1E67B2`) is the primary UI color for buttons, links, and icons. Teal (`#10BAB3`) is the secondary accent color for eyebrows, footer headings, and links. Green (`#81BF41`) is the tertiary color for supporting visuals and accents. This unifies the logo and the UI into a single cohesive color system.

### Placement Rules

- **Default position**: Upper-left corner of layouts, pages, and slides
- **Alternate position**: Upper-right (when layout demands; e.g., co-branded materials)
- **Clear space**: Minimum 1x the height of the logomark on all four sides — no text, imagery, or graphic elements may enter this zone
- **Minimum sizes**:
  - Horizontal variant: 120px wide (digital), 1.5 inches (print)
  - Logomark only: 32px wide (digital), 0.4 inches (print)
- **File formats**: Always use **SVG** for web and digital; PNG fallbacks at **2x resolution** minimum (e.g., if displayed at 120px, the PNG file should be 240px wide)

### Logo Don'ts

- **Never** stretch, skew, compress, or rotate the logo
- **Never** add drop shadows, outer glows, bevels, or any visual effects
- **Never** change the logo colors — the blue, green, and teal are fixed
- **Never** place the logo on busy, patterned, or photographically complex backgrounds without a solid or semi-transparent backing panel to ensure contrast
- **Never** recreate, redraw, or modify the logomark — always use approved asset files
- **Never** lock up the logo with other logos without brand team approval
- **Never** animate the logo without brand team approval
- **Never** use the logo smaller than the minimum size specifications

## 9. Photography & Imagery

### Guidelines

- **Diverse, real people**: Imagery must reflect the diversity of the communities we serve — varied ages, ethnicities, body types, and abilities
- **Warm, well-lit environments**: Natural lighting preferred; avoid harsh fluorescents or cold-toned clinical lighting
- **Welcoming clinical settings**: Research sites should feel approachable — clean but not sterile, professional but not intimidating
- **Human connection**: Prioritize images showing researcher-patient interaction, conversation, and care — not just equipment or empty rooms
- **Community context**: Include imagery of local neighborhoods, community settings, and real places where sites operate
- **Authentic moments**: Candid or semi-candid shots over posed studio photography

### Photography Treatments

| Treatment | Application |
|---|---|
| **Full-bleed** | Hero sections, feature images — no overlay needed if text is not placed on top |
| **Dark blue overlay** | 60–80% opacity dark blue (`#0D2949`) — for images with text overlay in hero sections and social |
| **Rounded corners** | 10px radius — when used as inline images within content sections |
| **Duotone** | Blue-500 + Dark blue-800 duotone filter — for stylized/editorial uses (sparingly) |

### Image Don'ts

- **No dark, clinical, or intimidating imagery** — avoid empty hospital corridors, ominous lab shots, or clinical equipment without human context
- **No all-white/sterile lab photos** without a human element — always include people
- **No stock photos with watermarks** or obviously staged, overly polished corporate poses
- **No images that imply fear or anxiety** about clinical research participation
- **No images of children** in clinical settings without explicit legal and ethical clearance
- **No competitor logos, branded materials, or identifiable competitor locations** in any imagery

## 10. Iconography

### Style Specifications

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Default size | 24 x 24px |
| Stroke width | 1.5px |
| Line caps | Rounded |
| Line joins | Rounded |
| Color | `currentColor` (inherits from parent element for easy theming) |
| Corner radius (on shapes) | 2px minimum |
| Grid | Icons designed on a 24px grid with 2px padding (20px live area) |

### Size Variants

| Name | Size | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| **Small** | 16px | Inline text icons, metadata, compact UI elements |
| **Default** | 20px | Standard UI — navigation, lists, form helpers |
| **Large** | 24px | Cards, feature sections, prominent UI elements |
| **Feature** | 32px | Feature blocks, landing page sections, empty states |

### Icon Usage Rules

- Always use SVG format for web — never rasterized icons
- Maintain consistent stroke width across all sizes (1.5px) — do not scale stroke with icon size
- Use `currentColor` so icons automatically match their text context
- Clinical/medical icon set is available in `assets/icons/`
- When pairing an icon with text, maintain 8px (space_2) gap between icon and label
- Icons in buttons are 20px, aligned vertically centered with button text
- Do not fill icons unless the filled variant is explicitly provided in the icon set
- Do not add decorative effects (shadows, gradients, glows) to icons
- Do not mix icon styles — all icons in a single view should use the same stroke weight and style

### Icon Color Guidelines

| Context | Icon Color |
|---|---|
| On white/light backgrounds | neutral-600 (`#4B5563`) or blue-500 (`#1E67B2`) |
| On dark/dark blue backgrounds | White (`#FFFFFF`) or teal-200 (`#9FE3E0`) |
| Interactive (clickable) | blue-500, with blue-600 on hover |
| Disabled | neutral-300 (`#D1D5DB`) |
| Status: success | `#10B981` |
| Status: warning | `#F59E0B` |
| Status: error | `#EF4444` |
| Status: info | `#3B82F6` |

## Quick Reference: Decision Matrix

When in doubt, use this matrix to make brand decisions quickly:

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| What color is this button? | **Blue.** Primary and CTA buttons all use blue-500. |
| What font for this heading? | Display, H1, H2? **Montserrat Bold.** H3-H5? **Montserrat Medium.** |
| What font for this body text? | Always **Noto Sans Regular** (or Medium/Semi-Bold for emphasis). |
| Is this heading Title Case? | **No.** Always sentence case. |
| Can I use ALL CAPS here? | Only for **Overline, Label, and Button** styles. |
| What background for this section? | Default: **white.** Alternating: **neutral-50.** Impact: **dark blue-800.** Brand: **blue-50.** |
| What color for this icon? | Light bg: **neutral-600 or blue-500.** Dark bg: **white or teal-200.** |
| Can I modify the logo? | **No.** Use approved files exactly as provided. |
| Photo needs text overlay? | Use **dark blue overlay at 60-80% opacity**, then white or teal text. |
